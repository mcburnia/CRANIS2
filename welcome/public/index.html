1. What changes when someone asks you to prove it

You already know your software works. The challenge is proving it.

Customers ask for Software Bills of Materials (SBOMs). Insurers ask how you manage vulnerabilities. Regulators expect documentation that reflects reality, not a point-in-time audit.

Most teams respond manually. They pull data from multiple tools, update documents, and prepare evidence under time pressure.

CRANIS2 replaces that with a continuous, evidence-driven view of your software.

It connects to your repositories, reads dependency metadata, and builds a live record of what your software contains, what risks exist, and how they are managed.

No spreadsheets. No audit scramble. No change to how your team works.

2. What you get immediately

  • A complete, current inventory of every dependency across your products
  • A live view of vulnerabilities, prioritised by actual risk
  • Up-to-date Software Bills of Materials (SBOMs) generated automatically
  • Licence exposure identified before it becomes a problem
  • A technical file that reflects what is actually happening in your software

This is not a report. It is a continuous view.

4. What this saves you

Most teams spend significant time assembling evidence that should already exist.

CRANIS2 removes that work.

  • Engineers stop maintaining spreadsheets and preparing for audits
  • Compliance work shifts from manual to automatic
  • Sales cycles shorten because evidence is available on demand

For a mid-sized product portfolio, this typically returns hundreds to thousands of engineering hours and reduces reliance on repeat consulting engagements.

You still bring in specialists where needed. The difference is you start from evidence, not from scratch.

11. Security and data handling

  • CRANIS2 never writes to your repositories
  • CRANIS2 never copies or stores your source code
  • Dependency metadata is used to identify components
  • For older languages, limited inspection is used solely for component identification
  • Intellectual property remains in your environment

Platform security includes:

  • Post-quantum hybrid signing (Ed25519 + ML-DSA-65)
  • AES-256-GCM encryption with HKDF key derivation
  • Automated key rotation
  • European hosting and sovereignty alignment
